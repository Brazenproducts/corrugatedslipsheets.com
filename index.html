Fleet Management Perspective

The Economics of Building a PP Slip Sheet Fleet

Every distribution operation that uses pallet tier sheets is already running some form of a slip sheet program. The question is whether that program is managed as a strategic asset or treated as a line-item consumable. For most operations still relying on single-use corrugated cardboard tier sheets, the answer is the latter — and it is costing more than the unit pricing suggests.

The shift to PP corrugated slip sheets in bulk — bought in pallet quantities and managed as a returnable fleet — is not a product upgrade. It is a supply chain management decision. It changes how you procure, track, and account for a component that touches every pallet you ship. And when that decision is made at scale, the financial case is straightforward.

Understanding the 80%+ Per-Use Cost Reduction

The 80% figure is not a projection — it is the result of straightforward arithmetic. A corrugated PP slip sheet from Elipacko costs more per unit than a single cardboard tier sheet. That is not disputed. The calculation changes when you account for reuse. A PP slip sheet rated for 30 to 50 cycles, bought in bulk at program pricing, delivers a per-trip cost that is a fraction of a single-use cardboard tier sheet purchased at equivalent volume.

Across two operating seasons — a commonly used evaluation window that accounts for initial fleet build-out and ramp — the PP slip sheet fleet's total cost of ownership per pallet trip drops by more than 80% compared to running single-use cardboard. That figure accounts for the full fleet investment amortized across trips. It does not even account for the ancillary cost savings: reduced disposal fees, eliminated cardboard compaction labor, reduced procurement overhead from constant cardboard reorders, and the avoided cost of product damage claims attributable to weakened cardboard tier support.

Buying in Pallet Quantities: What It Means for Operations

Elipacko supplies PP corrugated slip sheets in full pallet quantities. Buying at pallet scale accomplishes several things simultaneously. It drives unit cost down, which improves the economics of the fleet build. It ensures the entire initial fleet is produced from the same batch specification — consistent resin, consistent gauge, consistent dimensions — so every sheet in your fleet performs identically. And it simplifies procurement to a periodic event rather than an ongoing consumable purchasing cycle.

For large operations, the pallet-quantity purchasing model means forecasting slip sheet consumption once per season or once per year, placing a program order, and managing from a known fleet inventory. That is fundamentally different from the rolling cardboard reorder model, where procurement is reactive and inconsistent supply creates operational variability.

Custom Print for Fleet Management and Ownership Marking

One of the persistent challenges of operating a reusable slip sheet fleet in a multi-party supply chain is sheet recovery. When PP slip sheets travel through distribution networks — to retail DCs, to co-manufacturers, to third-party logistics providers — sheets that are not clearly identified as owned assets tend not to come back. Elipacko offers custom print programs that apply company names, logos, facility codes, or tracking identifiers directly to the PP corrugated surface.

The print is applied to the polypropylene surface in a way that survives repeated washing and handling. It is not a label that peels — it is integrated into the sheet surface. This makes ownership identification permanent and durable across the full operating life of the sheet. For supply chain operations running closed-loop pallet programs or working with supply chain partners under sheet-return agreements, custom print is a practical fleet management tool that protects the asset investment.

PP Corrugated Slip Sheets in Regulated Environments

Many of the operations most interested in PP corrugated slip sheets at fleet scale are operating in regulated environments — fresh produce, food processing, pharmaceutical distribution, and nutraceutical manufacturing. In these environments, the material compliance of every packaging component that contacts or is in proximity to product is a documented requirement.

Elipacko's PP corrugated slip sheets are produced from polypropylene resin formulated to meet the requirements of FDA 21 CFR 177.1520 — the standard governing polypropylene materials used in food-contact articles. This provides a documented compliance basis that cardboard cannot replicate, particularly for sheets that have already been in service and cannot be re-tested or re-certified. PP enters service compliant and remains compliant throughout its reuse life, as long as it is kept in serviceable condition through normal cleaning and inspection protocols.

Beyond FDA compliance, the PP surface supports the sanitization protocols required in FSMA-regulated facilities. Sheets can be cleaned with food-safe cleaning agents, rinsed, inspected, and returned to rotation. This documentation trail is valuable in audit contexts and supports the preventive control frameworks required under the Food Safety Modernization Act.

Fleet Sizing: How to Estimate Your PP Slip Sheet Program

Sizing a PP slip sheet fleet requires two inputs: your daily or weekly pallet volume by tier count, and your average turn cycle — the time between when a sheet goes out and when it returns. For a single-facility distribution operation, the sizing is straightforward. For multi-facility or multi-partner supply chains, the sizing calculation needs to account for sheets in transit and sheets held at partner facilities.

A conservative fleet sizing rule for initial programs is to plan for two to three times your peak-day sheet requirement. This buffers for turn time, sheets in washing and inspection, and sheets held at downstream facilities. As the fleet matures and turn cycles become predictable, the buffer can be adjusted. Elipacko can assist with fleet sizing calculations based on your pallet volume and supply chain configuration — contact our team with your annual pallet data to get a program recommendation.

Sustainability and Waste Reduction at Scale

Operating a PP corrugated slip sheet fleet materially reduces the waste generated by pallet tier sheet programs. A single PP sheet that replaces 30 to 50 cardboard tier sheets over its life eliminates 29 to 49 single-use cardboard units from the waste stream for every trip cycle completed. At fleet scale — thousands of sheets completing dozens of cycles each — the reduction in solid waste generation is substantial.

For operations with corporate sustainability commitments, packaging waste reduction targets, or supply chain partners requiring waste minimization documentation, transitioning from single-use cardboard to a reusable PP slip sheet fleet is a quantifiable, reportable improvement. The reduction in single-use material consumption is documented at the point of fleet conversion and trackable across each subsequent season.
